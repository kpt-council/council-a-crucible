# Potential Domains for the Council Runtime Framework

Based on discussion and operational observation, the framework appears
most useful in **contexts where complex questions benefit from multiple
cognitive perspectives**. Its design---separating analytical,
generative, experiential, and intuitive modes---supports structured
exploration of problems that are ambiguous, multi-dimensional, or
consequential.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Use Cases

### Strategic Planning and Decision Analysis

The framework supports examining decisions from analytical,
human-impact, and systems perspectives before committing to action.

### Research Ideation and Hypothesis Exploration

Multiple reasoning styles help generate, critique, and refine ideas
during early-stage conceptual work.

### Design, Innovation, and Product Development

Different personas naturally map to phases of innovation: idea
generation, critique, consequence mapping, and refinement.

### Complex Problem Framing

Useful when the problem itself is unclear and requires iterative
reframing before solutions are possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Creative and Meaning-Making Domains

### Creative Writing and Narrative Development

The framework supports shifting between structure, emotional depth,
pattern recognition, and intuition.

### Philosophical Inquiry and Reflective Dialogue

Well suited to exploratory conversations where the goal is deeper
understanding rather than immediate answers.

### Personal Reflection and Structured Thinking

Can act as a scaffold for individuals thinking through difficult
decisions or ambiguous life questions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Analytical and Professional Domains

### Policy Analysis and Governance

Multiple perspectives help surface tradeoffs, stakeholder impacts, and
systemic consequences.

### Risk Analysis and Scenario Planning

Structured dialogue between analytical and systems-oriented perspectives
helps anticipate complex outcomes.

### Legal Reasoning and Argumentation

Different voices can model competing interpretations, consequences, and
logical tests.

### Journalism and Investigative Analysis

Encourages examining narratives, evidence, and societal implications
from several angles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dialogue and Facilitation

### Conflict Mediation and Community Dialogue

Allows multiple perspectives to be expressed without collapsing the
discussion into a single dominant frame.

### Organizational Retrospectives and Post-Mortems

Separating analytical, systemic, and human perspectives can reduce blame
and improve insight into complex failures.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Educational Applications

### Teaching Critical Thinking

Students can see how different reasoning modes approach the same
problem.

### Interdisciplinary Learning

The framework helps bridge analytical, creative, and experiential modes
of understanding.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Underlying Pattern

Across all domains, the framework appears most valuable when problems
share three characteristics:

-   **Ambiguity** --- the question is not fully defined\
-   **Plural perspectives** --- multiple valid interpretations exist\
-   **Consequential outcomes** --- decisions affect people, systems, or
    narratives

In these environments, the framework functions as a **structured
multi-perspective dialogue system**, helping users explore a problem
space before settling on conclusions.
